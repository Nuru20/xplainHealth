1 in 3 Ugandan children is stunted. Most go undetected until it is too late.

Stunting — being too short for one's age — affects 28.4% of Ugandan children under five. Its consequences are irreversible: impaired brain development, lower school performance, reduced lifetime income, and the cycle repeating through the next generation.

The problem is not that stunting cannot be prevented. The problem is that at-risk children are not identified early enough for intervention to work. Village Health Team workers — Uganda's frontline community health cadre — have no practical tool for stunting risk screening.

The window closes at 24 months

Stunting that develops in the first 1,000 days is largely irreversible. Current systems miss children until they reach a health facility — often years too late.

📏

MUAC cannot detect stunting

The only community screening tool — the MUAC tape — measures acute wasting, not chronic stunting. Village health workers have no way to flag stunting risk.

📡

No data reaches the DHO

District Health Officers make resource allocation decisions with no real-time data on where stunting risk is highest in their district.

🌍

Northern Uganda most affected

Post-conflict communities in Northern Uganda have stunting rates exceeding 40% — but receive interventions based on historical averages, not live data.

Our Solution

A two-tier AI system — from village to district

Mwana AI connects community screening by VHT workers to real-time district-level monitoring — all powered by open-source explainable AI.

📱
VHT Community Screening App
Six-input mobile Progressive Web App for Village Health Team workers. Works completely offline after first load. The XGBoost model runs entirely in the browser — no server, no connectivity required. Installs on any Android phone from the browser in one tap. MUAC acts as a clinical safety override for severe acute malnutrition.
Offline-first PWA 6 inputs Android MUAC override Open source

Research & Clinical Dashboard
Full 19-input stunting risk predictor for researchers, clinicians, and District Health Officers. Shows SHAP waterfall charts explaining every prediction in plain language, a risk gauge, and a population monitoring tab displaying live VHT screenings from across the district in real time.

Shared Live Database
Both tools write every screening to a single Supabase PostgreSQL database. The source column identifies whether each record came from a VHT community visit or a clinical assessment. District Health Officers see all screenings from both tools in the research dashboard Tab 2 — updated in real time.

How It Works

From village visit to district intelligence

1
VHT screens child
Village Health Team worker opens the app on their phone. Enters 6 simple inputs — child age, sex, MUAC, whether mother can read, children under 5, and distance to clinic.
→
2
AI predicts risk
XGBoost model runs in the browser — no internet needed. Shows GREEN, YELLOW, or RED result instantly. MUAC below 115mm always triggers RED regardless of the model.
→
3
Record saved
When internet is available the screening saves to the shared database. District, VHT code, risk level, and probability are all stored — building a live picture of risk across the community.
→
4
DHO sees patterns
The District Health Officer opens the Research Dashboard Tab 2. Sees total screenings, high-risk percentages, district breakdowns, and trends — enabling targeted resource allocation.
